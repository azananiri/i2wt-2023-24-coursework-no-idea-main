August 24-29, 1921

Archduke Francis Ferdinand was assassinated.

Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia, launching World War I.

Germany invades both Luxembourg and Belgium. France invades Alsace. British forces arrive in France. Great Britain, Russia, Italy, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Rhodesia, Romania, Greece, France, Belgium, the United States, Canada, Serbia, India, Portugal, Montenegro, and Poland would finally join forces to oppose Germany.

Austria-Hungary invades Russian territory.

Germany establishes a naval blockade of Great Britain.The First Battle of Marne.

The Allied soldiers land on the Ottoman Empire's Gallipoli Peninsula.

A German submarine wrecks the passenger liner Lusitania while sailing from New York to Liverpool, England, killing 128 Americans.

Italy declares war against Austria-Hungary.

Germany begins its attack on Verdun.

The Naval Battle of Jutland takes place between the British and German fleets.

The Allied onslaught begins the Battle of the Somme.

The Battle of Verdun ends with 550,000 French and 450,000

Germany resumes unrestricted submarine warfare, which was temporarily halted following the sinking of the Lusitania.

The United States ends diplomatic relations with Germany.

The United States declares war against Germany.

General John J. Pershing, the recently appointed leader of the American Expeditionary Forces, arrives in England with his staff.

The American combat forces arrive in France.

Russia concludes an armistice with Germany.

President Woodrow Wilson presents to Congress his outline of the Fourteen Points Required for Peace.

The Stars and Stripes publishes its inaugural issue in one thousand copies. Second Lieutenant Guy T. Viskniskki is the newspaper's first managing editor.

The Brest-Litovsk Treaty is signed by Russia and Germany.

Germany launches its final onslaught of the war.

American women selected to work as multilingual telephone operators for the AEF arrive in Europe.

The US forces triumph in the Battle of Cantigny, the first solo American operation.

At Chateau-Thierry, American forces halt a German attempt to cross the Marne River.

The Stars and Stripes have suspended the Sporting Page.

The American First Army strikes the Saint Mihiel salient.

The final offensive of the war begins when allied forces launch an attack on Muse-Argonne.

Germany signs the Armistice at Compiègne, effectively ending World War I.

Harold Ross takes over as editor of The Stars and Stripes.

British and American troops enter Germany.

The Stars and Stripes War Orphans Adoption Campaign has concluded, raising 123,047 francs and placing 3,444 orphans for adoption.

Sporting Page returns to The Stars and Stripes.

A peace conference begins in Paris.

The first anniversary of the Stars and Stripes. Circulation exceeds 500,000.

The draught of the League of Nations Covenant has been completed.

The final issue of The Stars and Stripes is released.

The Treaty of Versailles is signed by delegates from the Allies and Germany. The United States signed a treaty of guarantee, promising to support France in the event of an unprovoked attack by Germany.

The United States Senate fails to ratify the Treaty of Versailles.

The Treaty of Versailles takes effect.

The US Senate fails to ratify the Treaty of Versailles for the second time.

The United States concludes separate peace treaties with Germany, Austria, and Hungary.
