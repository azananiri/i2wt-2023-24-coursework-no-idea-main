Harry S. Truman
Harry S. Truman, the 33rd president of the United States, replaced Franklin D. Roosevelt after the latter died in April 1945. Truman, who led the country during the last months of World War II, is best remembered for his controversial decision to fire two atomic bombs against Japan in August 1945. Following the war, Truman was instrumental in implementing the Marshall Plan, which hastened Western Europe's economic recovery.
